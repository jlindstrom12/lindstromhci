App Design and Development: Cafe 23

Date: January - May 2023

Back

Overview

    My Application Design and Development class revolved around learning ruby on rails and MVC. If you are unaware, MVC stands for Models, Views, and Controllers. We spent the majority of the semester building an app using MVC in ruby which was cafe 23. The goal was to develop an app for an imaginary cafe, cafe 23, to manage clocking in/clocking out, payroll, and all the information regarding stores, employees, shifts, and paygrades.

User Stories and ERD

Before coding anything we first had to understand the database and our users. Our three primary users are Admins, Managers, and Employees. All of which have different needs and wants. I considered all users and started off by creating a list of A, B, and C level needs for the app. Before we started to code first we had to understand the needs of our users. Our primary users are Managers, Employees, and Admins. All of the needs are too long to be listed here but they were detailed in this link: . For phase 1 we went through this document and considered all of the needs of our users by A level, B level, and C level. Models and Controllers

After gaining an understanding for all the function I intend to implement and the relationship between the entities in the database I could start coding. I began creating models for assignments, employees, and stores ensuring that I tested everything to make sure it worked as intended. I then finished my models creating shifts, pay grade, pay grade rates, jobs, and shift jobs. Once my models all had 100% test coverage I started on the controllers. The controllers would allow me to connect my models to the views I intended on making later. I created models for the entities that I wanted to create views for: Employees, shifts, stores, jobs, and xxx.
