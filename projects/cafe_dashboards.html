Cafe 23: Admin & Employee Centered Dashboard Design

Date: January - May 2023

Back

Overview

The second part of my Application Design and Development class involved using Figma and Human-Centered design principles to create mockup dashboards for Admins and Managers to address their needs. We will dive into their problems, desires, and painpoints as well as discuss how the dashboard mockups address these needs.

Personas

By understanding these user needs at different levels of priority, we ensured that our development efforts were focused on delivering an application that addresses the most critical requirements first while offering additional features for an enhanced user experience.

Dashboards

Dashboard Designed in Figma for Managers

Dashboard Designed in Figma for Admins

When designing these dashboard I had different thoughts in mind for Managers and Admins. For Managers I wanted to focus in on their store. This is why I highlighted important elements like the number of employees and info important to their store like shift coverage and payroll completion. I utilized red to go with the inital color scheme to draw attention to the most urgent areas.

For the Admin dashboard I took a different approach. I took it a little more broad focusing on the buisness as a whole as opposed to individual employees. To do this, I focused on the stores and the managers. Through the Admin dashboards they can see important elements like yearly store revenue, Store averages, and the employee statistics for each store.

Takeaways

Over the course of this project I learned about ruby on rails and using MVC for creating apps. I also learned more about concepts in web design such as reduction of cognitive load and the gulf of expectation and the gulf of evaluation. I intend on building upon the principles I learned in this class to further my understanding of app and web development.
